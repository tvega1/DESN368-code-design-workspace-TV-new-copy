Career Timeline

  • 1984
    Born in Akron, Ohio
    • LeBron Raymond James is born on December 30, 1984, to Gloria James.
    • He's raised by his single mother and shows early athletic talent.
  • 1999–2003
    High School Stardom
    • Attends St. Vincent–St. Mary High School in Akron, where he becomes a national basketball phenomenon.
    • Wins three state championships.
    • Named Gatorade National Player of the Year twice.
    • Appears on the cover of Sports Illustrated at age 17 as "The Chosen One."
  • 2003
    NBA Draft & Rookie of the Year
    • Selected #1 overall by the Cleveland Cavaliers in the 2003 NBA Draft.
    • Wins NBA Rookie of the Year (2004).
    • Instantly becomes the face of the franchise.
  • 2007
    First NBA Finals Appearance
    • Leads the Cavaliers to their first NBA Finals in franchise history.
    • Although they lose to the San Antonio Spurs, it marks LeBron's arrival as one of the league's top players.
  • 2010
    "The Decision"
    • LeBron announces his move to the Miami Heat on national TV.
    • This moment sparks huge media attention and controversy, but it begins a dominant era in Miami.
  • 2012
    First NBA Championship
    • Wins his first NBA title with the Miami Heat.
    • Named NBA Finals MVP.
    • Begins to silence critics who doubted his ability to win.
  • 2013
    Back-to-Back Titles
    • Wins his second consecutive championship with the Miami Heat.
    • Earns his fourth MVP award.
    • Cements his status as one of the greatest players in the game.
  • 2014
    Returns to Cleveland
    • LeBron announces: "I'm coming home."
    • He returns to the Cavaliers to bring a championship to his home state.
  • 2016
    Cleveland's First NBA Championship
    • Leads the Cavaliers to a historic comeback from a 3–1 deficit to defeat the Golden State Warriors.
    • Wins Finals MVP.
    • Brings Cleveland its first-ever NBA title.
  • 2018
    Joins the Los Angeles Lakers
    • Signs a multi-year contract with the Lakers, starting a new chapter in his career and life in Los Angeles.
  • 2020
    Fourth NBA Championship
    • Leads the Lakers to an NBA Championship inside the Orlando Bubble during the COVID-19 pandemic.
    • Wins his fourth Finals MVP.
    • Becomes one of the few players to win titles with three different teams.
  • 2023
    All-Time Scoring Record
    • Passes Kareem Abdul-Jabbar to become the NBA's all-time leading scorer with more than 38,390 points.
    • A historic milestone celebrating two decades of dominance.
  • 2024–Present
    Legacy Beyond Basketball
    • Still playing at an elite level in his 21st season.
    • Continues his LeBron James Family Foundation work.
    • Runs the I PROMISE School for at-risk youth in Akron.
    • Expands his influence in film, fashion, and social justice.

Overview

LeBron Raymone James Sr. is a four-time NBA champion, four-time MVP, and the league's all-time scoring leader. His journey spans the Cavaliers, Heat, Cavaliers (return), and Lakers—two decades of sustained excellence on and off the court.
