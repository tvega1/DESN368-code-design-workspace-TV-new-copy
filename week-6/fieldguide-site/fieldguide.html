The Basics

The Overworld: Your First Steps

Welcome to the Overworld, the primary dimension in Minecraft. This is where you'll spawn, build, and begin your survival journey. The landscape varies from dense forests to sprawling plains, from towering mountains to vast oceans.

Your first priority is to gather wood and establish shelter before nightfall. The Overworld is beautiful during the day but dangerous once the sun sets.

Progression

Tools & Crafting: Building Your Foundation

Crafting grid placeholder

Tools are the backbone of your Minecraft journey. Each tool type — pickaxe, shovel, axe, hoe, and sword — serves a unique purpose. The more advanced your materials, the faster and more durable your tools become.

Start with wooden tools and quickly upgrade to stone or iron for efficiency. You can only mine certain ores with higher-tier pickaxes; using the wrong one destroys the block without a drop.

Wood → Stone → Iron → Diamond → Netherite

Threat Assessment

Mobs: Friends and Foes

Tip: Use fences and lighting to keep your base mob-free.

Mobs are living creatures in Minecraft. Passive mobs like cows and sheep provide resources. Hostile mobs like creepers and skeletons spawn at night and in dark places. Learn to identify threats and manage your environment.

Resource Strategy

Mining: The Depths of Discovery

Ore depth chart placeholder

Pro Tip: Smelt ores in a furnace for ingots before crafting.

Mining is the primary way to gather valuable resources like iron, diamonds, and rare materials. Dig strategically, always carry torches, and never dig straight down or up. Follow ore veins and work your way deeper for better treasures.

Sustainability

Farming: Food and Sustainability

Establish a farm early to ensure a steady food supply. Wheat, potatoes, and carrots are the easiest crops to grow. Plant seeds, water them, and harvest when ready. Sustainable food production keeps your hunger bar full during long expeditions.

Appendix

Subsection 1 — Tips & Takeaways

  • Place torches on the right going in; left coming back to avoid getting lost
  • Carry a crafting table and furnace in your kit
  • Sleep to skip night and reset spawn point
Appendix

Subsection 2 — Resources

  • Village trading for early gear
  • Map + compass to mark home base
  • Bucket + water: universal safety tool
